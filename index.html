Frequently Asked Questions

Curious about Sheep?

Q: Do sheep have the mental capacity to conceive of spatial relations?
A: Actually sheep have been shown to be able remember their way around a maze, and researchers in Australia have shown that they can improve their times through a maze and retain this knowledge for up to six week. From http://www.sheep101.info/stupidsheep.html

Q: Wow! Give this facility with way-finding I bet sheep have proven really useful in exploration. Is that right?
A: Yes, Sheep have been key players in 15th and 16th century European exploration. For example, Columbus and other early Spanish explorers were financed by profits from the wool industry owned by Queen Isabella.

Q: Fascinating, so in addition to spatial skills, sheep are financial whizes!. Did sheep just pay for trips or did they get to join these voyages as well?
A: Actually, in 1493 Columbus brought along sheep when he sailed the second time to the New World. Unfortunately Columbus -- being the speciest that he was -- mostly viewed the sheep as a "walking food supply." http://www.sheep101.info/history.html

Q: How unfortunate! Was there a reaction to this, for example, did sheep come together and train for combat?
A: Yes. In some countries this has evolved into a tradition of training young sheep to fight. Being the clever creatures that they are, they disguise this activity as being part of celebratory events such as the Festival of Sacrifice. However, all sheep involved in the Sheep Shilling project are avowed pacifists. http://sheep101.info/interestingfacts.html
